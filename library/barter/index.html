Why Barter?

Because coin is not the only measure of value. Time, skill, surplus, care, and wit all count. The aim is simple: neighbors helping neighbors while keeping the dignity of both the giver and the receiver intact.

Promise: We will never require payment for rescue, food, or safety. Barter is an invitation to participate ‚Äî not a gate to keep anyone out.

How It Works

  1. Offer something you have: time, skill, item, or surplus.
  2. Ask for what you need: repair help, tutoring, a ride, a warm meal.
  3. Match with a steward. If nothing matches today, a steward will hold the offer until it does.
  4. Honor the exchange with a handshake or mark (the wax seal stamp is for fun; kids love it).

Examples

  • Fix two leaky faucets ‚Üí Receive two weeks of school lunches covered for a family in need.
  • Donate a cord of wood ‚Üí Get help splitting and stacking yours before the first freeze.
  • Teach a Saturday art class ‚Üí Borrow a sewing machine and lessons for your kid.

  • Never barter what you cannot afford to lose.
